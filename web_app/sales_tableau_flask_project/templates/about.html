The Project Involves

This project focuses on analyzing how product placement inside retail stores affects consumer buying behavior and overall sales performance. By examining data related to product categories, shelf positions, foot traffic and pricing, the project aims to identify patterns that influence purchasing decisions.

The first stage of the project involves data collection, where sales information and product positioning data are gathered from retail environments. These datasets provide insights into which products perform better when placed in specific areas such as front displays, aisles or end caps.

The second stage is data preparation and transformation. During this process, raw datasets are cleaned, structured and organized so that they can be analyzed effectively. Data preparation ensures accuracy and reliability before performing any visualization or analytical operations.

Finally, the project uses data visualization with Tableau to transform complex datasets into interactive dashboards and stories. These visualizations allow decision-makers to quickly identify trends, compare product performance and optimize store layouts to improve customer engagement and maximize sales.
